Dynamic Training Plan

General Warm-Up Routine (Approx. 15-20 Minutes)

The goal is to raise core body temperature, increase blood flow to muscles, activate key movement patterns, and prepare the nervous system for the specific work of the day.

Group 1: Sprints & Jumps (Elementary, Secondary Sprints, Secondary LJ/TJ)

This group needs to prepare for high-velocity, explosive movements. The focus is on dynamic mobility and neuromuscular activation.

  1. Pulse Raiser (3-5 mins):
    • 2-3 laps of an open field at an easy jog.
    • Incorporate variety: Side Shuffles, Carioca, Skipping, light jogging backwards.
  2. Dynamic Mobility (5-7 mins): (Perform each for ~10m)
    • Leg Swings: 10x each leg (forward/backward and side-to-side).
      • Stand tall, using a wall or fence for balance.
      • Swing one leg forward and backward, keeping it straight but relaxed.
      • Then, swing the same leg across your body and out to the side.
      • Focus on a smooth, controlled motion from the hip.
    • Walking Knee Hugs: Pull knee to chest, hold for a step.
      • Step forward and bring your opposite knee up toward your chest.
      • Grasp the knee with both hands and gently pull it closer.
      • Hold for a moment, focusing on balance and stretching the glute.
      • Release and step into the next hug.
    • Walking Quad Stretch: Grab ankle and pull heel to glute.
      • Take a step and then grab the ankle of your back foot.
      • Gently pull your heel towards your glute, feeling a stretch in the front of your thigh.
      • Keep your torso upright and hips pushed slightly forward.
      • Release, take a step, and repeat with the other leg.
    • Walking Lunges with a Twist: Step into a lunge, twist torso over the front leg.
      • Step forward into a lunge, keeping your front knee aligned with your ankle.
      • In the lunge position, twist your torso over your front leg.
      • Return to center, push off the front foot, and step into the next lunge.
    • Open and Close the Gate: Open the hips for more mobility.
      • Open: Stand tall, lift one knee to hip height, and rotate it outwards, as if stepping over a hurdle.
      • Close: From the outside, bring the knee back to the center and down.
      • Focus on controlled movement from the hip joint.
  3. Activation & Drills (5-7 mins): (Perform each for ~20 yards)
    • A-March/A-Skip: Focus on posture and striking under the hips.
      • March: March forward, bringing your knee to hip height (90-degree angle). Keep your ankle dorsiflexed (toes up). Drive the opposite arm up.
      • Skip: Add a small hop to the marching motion. The focus is on a quick, rhythmic "pop" off the ground.
    • B-Skip: Emphasizes hamstring extension and pulling action.
      • Perform an A-Skip, but at the peak of the knee lift, extend your lower leg forward.
      • Actively pull the leg back down and under your hip, striking the ground in a pawing motion.
    • Butt Kicks & High Knees: For turnover and frequency.
      • Butt Kicks: Run forward, focusing on bringing your heels directly up towards your glutes. Keep your thighs vertical.
      • High Knees: Run forward, focusing on lifting your knees to hip height. Stay on the balls of your feet.
    • Straight Leg Bounds: For hamstring readiness.
      • Keeping your legs relatively straight, bound forward by pushing off forcefully from your toes.
      • The motion should feel like a stiff-legged run, focusing on hamstring engagement as you pull your leg through.
    • Dribble Series: For coordination and active ground contact.
      • Ankle Dribbles (2x20m): Walk or jog forward, lifting one foot in a circular motion just over the opposite ankle. Strike the ground heel-first in a "down and back" motion.
      • Calf Dribbles (2x20m): Increase the height of the circular motion to the midpoint of the opposite calf.
      • Knee Dribbles (2x20m): Lift the foot in a circular motion over the opposite knee, mimicking a full running cycle in a compressed form.
  4. Build-Ups (2-3 mins):
    • 3-4 x 60-80m strides on the field.
    • Gradually increase speed with each one: 60% -> 70% -> 80% -> 85% effort. Focus on relaxed, fast form.

Group 2: Distance (Middle & Long Distance)

This group needs to prepare the aerobic system while also ensuring good mechanics and mobility for efficient running.

  1. Pulse Raiser (5-10 mins):
    • Longer continuous jog than the sprint group (800m-1600m) at a very easy, conversational pace.
  2. Dynamic Mobility (5 mins):
    • Same routine as the Sprints & Jumps group (Leg Swings, Lunges, etc.). Hip mobility is critical for running economy. (See Group 1 for detailed instructions).
  3. Activation & Drills (3-5 mins): (Perform each for ~30-40 yards)
    • A-Skips, High Knees, Butt Kicks: The focus here is less on explosive power and more on rhythm and efficient movement. (See Group 1 for detailed instructions).
    • Carioca: Move sideways by crossing one foot in front of the other, then behind. Keep your hips facing forward and rotate your torso.
    • Side Shuffles: Stay in a low athletic stance and shuffle sideways without clicking your heels together.
    • Dribble Series: Focus on light, quick, and efficient foot contact. (See Group 1 for detailed instructions).
  4. Build-Ups (2-3 mins):
    • 4-6 x 100m strides on a flat stretch of road or field.
    • Focus on feeling smooth, light, and efficient, building to about 5k race pace effort.

Group 3: Throws

This group needs to prepare for powerful, full-body rotational movements. The focus is on joint mobility (shoulders, hips, spine) and explosive activation.

  1. Pulse Raiser (3-5 mins):
    • 1-2 laps easy jog, followed by skipping and light bounding.
  2. Dynamic Mobility (7-10 mins): This is the most critical part for them.
    • Arm Circles: Forward and backward, small to large.
      • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart and extend arms to the sides.
      • Make small circles, gradually increasing to large circles, then reverse direction.
    • Trunk Twists: Feet planted, rotating the torso.
      • Stand with feet slightly wider than shoulder-width, with a slight bend in the knees.
      • Rotate your torso from side to side in a controlled manner. Allow your arms to swing freely.
    • Deep Squat Hold: Sit in a full-depth bodyweight squat to open the hips.
      • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointed slightly out.
      • Lower your hips as if sitting in a chair, going as deep as you can comfortably.
      • Keep your chest up and back straight. Use your elbows to gently push your knees out. Hold for 20-30 seconds.
    • Spiderman Lunge with T-Spine Rotation: Lunge, place hand on ground, rotate chest to the sky.
      • Step into a deep lunge, placing both hands on the ground inside your front foot.
      • Lift the hand on the same side as your front leg and rotate your chest towards the ceiling.
      • Follow your hand with your eyes. Return to the start and switch sides.
    • Leg Swings & Hurdle Mobility: Same as other groups. (See Group 1 for detailed instructions).
  3. Activation & Drills (3-5 mins):
    • Standing Long Jumps: 3-5 jumps for explosive power.
    • Backward Jumps & Stick (T1): For deceleration strength.
    • "Mock Throws": Use a soccer/volleyball or just their body to go through the throwing motion at 50% speed to warm up the specific pattern.
  4. Build-Ups (1-2 mins):
    • 2-3 x 20-30m sprints to get the full body firing.

General Cool-Down Routine (Approx. 10-15 Minutes)

The goal is to gradually lower the heart rate, clear metabolic waste products, and improve flexibility by stretching warm muscles. This is largely the same for all groups, with specific stretches emphasized.

  1. Light Aerobic Activity (3-5 mins):
    • 1-2 laps of very slow jogging, followed by walking. This helps the body transition from a state of work to rest.
  2. Static Stretching (7-10 mins):
    • Hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds. Breathing should be deep and relaxed. Never stretch to the point of pain.
    • All Groups:
      • Hamstring Stretch (seated or standing)
      • Quad Stretch (standing or lying)
      • Calf Stretch (against a wall or post)
      • Glute/Piriformis Stretch (Pigeon Pose or Figure-4)
      • Hip Flexor Stretch (Kneeling Lunge)
    • Throwers - Additional Focus:
      • Chest/Pec Stretch (in a doorway or holding hands behind back)
      • Shoulder/Triceps Stretch (across the body and overhead)
      • Lat Stretch (reaching overhead and to the side)

This provides a solid, evidence-based framework for our warm-ups and cool-downs, Coach. We can adjust the time and specific exercises based on the main workout of the day and how the athletes are feeling.
